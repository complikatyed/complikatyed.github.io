From English Teacher to Web Developer

My favorite part of teaching (aside from talking books with my 7th graders, which I still miss) was imagining new ways to present my curriculum. Who knew Google Docs could be the tech equivalent of a gateway drug?

Moving my students to on-line writing let me monitor and comment on their work across the writing process. Customized Google forms quickly became my standard practice and assessment tools, and my class Google site brought together vocabulary, grammar, literature, and writing. At that point it just made sense to start taping videos and try flipping my classroom altogether.

I left the classroom to train as a developer when I realized that designing and creating Google-based tech tools had taken over the bulk of my prep time (and become the most fascinating aspect of my job).

While I miss the excitement of my middle school students, I'm completely in love with the challenges of full-stack coding.

Everything Clicks at Nashville CLICK

Designing and creating technology tools had taken over the bulk of my prep time (and become the most fascinating aspect of my job), but I wasn't brave enough to leave the classroom, yet.

Nashville CLICK, an event that paired teachers with working developers for a day of coding, gave me the confidence I needed to take the next step. I was finally ready to create teaching and learning tools beyond the limits of Google Apps for Education.

Two months later I quit my job at University School of Nashville and decided to apply to NSS.
